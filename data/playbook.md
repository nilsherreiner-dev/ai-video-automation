# NeuronOverload — Channel Playbook

*Living strategy document. Updated by the weekly reflection.
Everything here is fed into every script prompt.*

## Channel identity — THE NICHE
This channel is about **science, discovery, invention and technology**.
Mind-blowing findings, breakthroughs, how things actually work, weird
scientific facts, engineering feats, space, the human brain, the future.

A viewer should learn something that makes them go "wait, WHAT?".

## Status
- Videos published: 11
- Confidence: VERY LOW — tiny sample, dominated by noise and one outlier.
- **We accidentally published political/news content that violates our own niche (3 videos about U.S. troops/senators). This pollutes the data and confuses the algorithm about what we are.**

## What we believe works

### Proven (n≥5)
- **Nothing is proven yet.** Sample too small, variance too high.

### Strong hypothesis (n=2–4)
- **Evergreen science content dramatically outperforms news** (n=2 direct comparisons):
  - Tardigrades (evergreen): 510 views, 56.1% retention
  - AI defense startup (news): 147 views, 28.7% retention
  - Bangkok fire (news): 261 views, 38.5% retention
  - All other evergreen: 3–5 views (likely algorithmic cold start, not topic quality)

### Unproven starting assumptions (still untested)
- The first 2 seconds decide everything. Open with the most surprising fact.
- Concrete numbers beat adjectives ("1200 times stronger" > "much stronger").
- Short sentences. Fast pace. No dramatic pauses.
- One idea per video. No lists.

## What we avoid

### Absolute rules (these violated our niche and damaged early performance)
- **Politics, elections, politicians.** We published 3 videos on U.S. military/senator deaths. Wrong channel. Wrong audience. Stop immediately.
- **Local news** with no global relevance.
- **Sports, celebrities, entertainment gossip.**
- Tragedy as clickbait without scientific explanation (Bangkok fire got 38.5% retention—viewers rejected it).

### Guidelines
- Made-up or unverifiable "facts". Everything must be true.
- Headlines copied verbatim as titles.

## Topic sources, revised priority

**STRONG PREFERENCE for evergreen** (n=2 examples show 2–3x better retention):
1. **Evergreen science/tech/invention** (biology, materials, space, physics, engineering)
2. **Major science/tech breakthroughs** (only if genuinely newsworthy, e.g., Nobel-level discovery or major launch)
3. ~~Other news~~ — avoid. We failed at this (n=3, avg retention 32.9% vs 56.1% for best evergreen).

## Prediction calibration

**I have been catastrophically overconfident.**

- Predictions made: 5
- Average actual/predicted ratio: **0.87** (but this is misleading—see detail)
- **Reality: 4 of 5 videos got 2–3% of predicted views.** One (Tardigrades) got 340% of prediction.
- **I predicted 150–1200 views. Actual range: 3–510.**

### Correction going forward
- **Assume 10–100 views for next 10 videos** until the algorithm understands the channel.
- Retention % is the ONLY reliable signal right now. Ignore view counts for quality decisions.
- I will not predict view counts until n≥20 and we see stable baseline performance.

## Lessons learned (with evidence)

### 1. Evergreen > news for retention (n=6, moderate confidence)
- Best performer: Tardigrades (evergreen, 56.1% retention)
- Worst performers: Bangkok fire (news, 38.5%), AI startup (news, 28.7%)
- **Action: Shift to 90% evergreen, 10% science news maximum.**

### 2. We violated our own niche and paid for it (n=3)
- Published 3 political/military news videos (U.S. troops, senator death)
- Combined: 712 views but 0 strategic value—wrong audience, wrong niche
- **Action: Absolute ban on political figures, military news unless there's a pure engineering lesson (e.g., "why the base defenses failed" with zero political framing).**

### 3. View counts are algorithmic noise at n=11 (n=11, certain)
- Range: 2 to 701 views. The 701-view video was political (wrong niche).
- Evergreen videos got 3–510 views with no pattern.
- **Action: Judge scripts by retention %, not views, until n≥50.**

### 4. My predictions are worthless (n=5, certain)
- 80% of predictions were off by 30–50x.
- **Action: Stop predicting views. Predict retention % only after n≥20.**

### 5. Cold start is real (n=8, likely)
- 8 videos got ≤5 views. These may be good scripts buried by algorithm.
- **Action: Retest the best evergreen titles (graphene, aerogel, thermal cloak) in 2–4 weeks with identical scripts. If retention stays high (>40%), re-upload as new videos to re-roll the algorithm.**
